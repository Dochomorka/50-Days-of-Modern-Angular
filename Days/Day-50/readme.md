### **Core Concepts**

#### **1. The Architectural Big Picture**
Looking back at your workspace, every file now has a purpose and a place within the **Universal Angular Application Architecture**. By separating your code into **Core**, **Layout**, **UI**, **Pattern**, and **Feature**, you have created a system that remains maintainable and extendable, regardless of how large it grows.

#### **2. Mechanical Boundaries vs. Hope**
The most significant shift in your development process is the transition from **"Hope-Based Architecture"** to **Mechanical Boundaries**. By using **ESLint boundaries**, you no longer hope that reviewers catch architectural slips; the tooling acts as a "friendly senior reviewer" that enforces your **One-Way Dependency Graph** automatically.

#### **3. The "Black Box" Feature**
You have mastered treating **Lazy Features** as isolated black boxes. This allows you to evolve individual parts of your application independently, making your codebase resilient to the ever-changing requirements of the frontend landscape.

### **Hands-on: The Final Showcase**

Today, your task is to perform a final audit of your **Example Project Repository**. This repository represents the "End Reality" of the challenge— a clean, scaffolded skeleton ready for any enterprise requirement.

1.  **Dependency Audit**: Run your `analyze:deps` script one last time. Ensure your graph shows a clear "left-to-right" flow with **zero cycles**.
2.  **Performance Verification**: Use the Chrome DevTools **Performance Tab** to verify that your **Hydration** and **SSR** setup results in a minimal "Evaluate Script" time.
3.  **Budget Check**: Ensure your production build passes all **Bundle Budgets**, proving your use of **Standalone Components** and **`@defer`** has kept the app lean.
